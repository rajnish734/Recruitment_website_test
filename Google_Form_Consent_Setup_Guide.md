### FORM SECTION 2: Study Summary
**Field Type:** Section (with description text)

**Section Title:**
```
Study Summary
```

**Section Description:**
```
We are asking you to take part in a study about tracking change in focus levels in real time. We want to test whether an EEG headband can assess the change in focus levels of the participants with high levels of accuracy.

• Purpose: We are testing whether a wearable headband called the FRENZ brainband can measure cognitive load (how mentally demanding a task feels) in real time while people are doing common tasks (e.g., short games, puzzles, reading).

• What you'll do (single visit, about 90–100 minutes):
  - Wear the FRENZ headband (it records brain, heart, and head position signals).
  - Do short activities (some easy, some harder), answer quick questions about your focus, and complete short questionnaires about workload.

• Risks: Minimal. Mild skin/pressure discomfort from the headband, possible boredom or frustration during tasks.

• Benefits: You may gain some insight into how focus levels can change with work. This study could help create better tools to monitor attention and workload in schools, work, and training.

• Alternatives: You can choose not to take part.

• Voluntary: Your choice to join or not will not affect your grades, job prospects, or medical care.

Your decision will not affect your grades, job, medical care, or relationship with Dartmouth. Please ask any questions you have.
```

### FORM SECTION 5: Study Procedures
**Field Type:** Section (with description text)

**Section Title:**
```
What does this study involve? If you take part in this study, what activities will be done only for research purposes?
```

**Section Description:**
```
Your participation lasts one visit of about 90–100 minutes. You will:

• Wear the FRENZ brainband while seated at a computer.

• Baseline: Sit quietly with eyes open and then closed for short periods.

• On-task activities: Play brief memory/logic/maths games, relax with breathing/meditation, and experience short planned interruptions (for example, a fake error message) that may change your focus.

• Questionnaires: After activities, complete short ratings about how mentally demanding the tasks felt:
  - NASA-TLX (6 quick items about how difficult the task was)
  - Subjective Mental Effort Questionnaire (SMEQ) (1 quick item about how difficult the task was)
  - Quick focus ratings: You'll rate your focus before/after interruptions on a 1–10 scale.

Experimental procedures: Wearing the brainband and all task/questionnaire procedures are for research purposes.
```

### FORM SECTION 10: Privacy Protection
**Field Type:** Section (with description text)

**Section Title:**
```
How will your privacy be protected?
```

**Section Description:**
```
The information collected as data for this study includes:

• Signals recorded by the FRENZ brainband (brain activity/EEG, heart/PPG, motion/IMU)

• Task events, reaction times, scores, and your answers to short questionnaires and surveys.

• Simple demographics (e.g., age range, major) and a short "how prepared/tired do you feel" rating

How we protect it:

• Your data is labeled with a study ID code (not your name).

• Files are stored on encrypted Dartmouth systems with password protection and limited access to authorized study staff only.

• Any transfer of data outside the lab uses encrypted methods.

• Identifiers (like your name or email), if collected for scheduling, are stored separately from research data.

• Identifiable data will be destroyed when it is no longer needed for scheduling or follow-up.

• We will not send raw data or individual artifacts by email.

• Data will be transferred directly from survey platforms or institutional databases to secure Google Drive folders. All transfers will use Dartmouth IT–recommended methods (e.g., VPN, encryption) to protect data.

We are careful to protect the identities of the people in this study. We also keep the information collected for this study secure and confidential.
```

## GETTING THE FORM LINK

Once your form is complete:

1. Click the "Send" button (top right)
2. Click the link icon (🔗)
3. Copy the form URL
4. Extract the form ID (the part between `/d/e/` and `/viewform`)
   - Example: `https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1ABC123xyz/viewform`
   - Form ID: `1ABC123xyz`
5. Replace `CONSENT_FORM_LINK` in your `index.html` with this form ID

## NOTES FOR IRB SUBMISSION

- Digital consent via Google Forms is acceptable if your IRB approves
- Typed name = digital signature
- Checkboxes = acknowledgment of understanding
- Automatic timestamp = consent date/time record
- Google Sheets responses = consent records (store securely per IRB requirements)

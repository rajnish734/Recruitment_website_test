Introduction to the Real-Time Focus Tracking Project

We are recruiting participants for our large scale study on real time focus tracking. The purpose of the study is to track the focus of a participant in real time while they perform a number of activities on an iPad while wearing a wearable EEG device called FRENZ headband. The activities will include various games, academic tasks and relaxation exercises that will require varying cognitive load from the user.

This is a sample recruitment page for a system I built in a previous research project. The study focused on modulating participants' cognitive load while their EEG data was streamed from a wearable EEG device. I developed the study design and created all activities as a series of interactive games. This is not an actual recruitment page, and is not associated with any study.

Games You Will Play

During the study, you will participate in various cognitive games designed to measure focus and attention. Explore the games below:

Memory Matrix Game

Memorize the positions of highlighted squares on a grid and tap the squares you remember. Difficulty increases as you progress.

Raindrop Game

Solve simple math problems inside the blue raindrops falling from the sky before they reach the water level. Difficulty increases with correct answers.

Sliding Tiles Game

Solve simple math problems appearing inside two tiles. Mentally solve the problems and slide the tile with the lower answer into the tile with the higher answer using the left/right arrow buttons.

N-Back Games (N = 1, 2, 3)

Challenge your working memory by memorizing shapes and sequences. In N-Back 1, match the current shape with the one from 1 instant ago. In N-Back 2, match with 2 instants ago, and in N-Back 3, match with 3 instants ago.

Color Match Game

Two tiles appear side by side with color names. Press YES if the color name in the left tile matches the color of the letters on the right tile, otherwise press NO. The game gets faster as you advance with levels.

We will have additional activities that would require reading, story - telling and relaxation activities. Instructions will be available for these simpler activities.
